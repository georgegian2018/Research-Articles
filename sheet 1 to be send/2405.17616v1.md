## ABSTRACT

This paper presents the design and characterization of a rectangular microstrip patch antenna array optimized for operation within the Ku-band frequency range.

**Key Design Features:**
- Impedance-matched to 50Ω
- Microstrip line feeding mechanism for excitation
- Compact dimensions: 29.5 × 7 mm overall area
- Substrate material: RO3003

**Substrate Specifications:**
- Dielectric constant: 3
- Loss tangent: 0.0009
- Thickness: 1.574 mm
- Backed by conducting ground plane

**Array Configuration:**
- Six radiating patch elements
- Linear arrangement
- Positioned on top of substrate

**Performance Characteristics:**
- Resonant frequency: 18 GHz
- -10 dB impedance bandwidth: 700 MHz
- High gain: 7.51 dBi
- Well-suited for 5G and future communication systems

**Key Advantages:**
- Compact form factor
- Cost-effectiveness
- Broad impedance coverage
- Broad radiation coverage
- Potential for 5G and beyond applications

**Keywords:** RO3003, six elements, Radiofrequency, Ku-band, radiations

### 1.4 Literature Review of Beamforming Techniques

**Commonly Explored Techniques:**

**Patch Width Expansion Method:**
- Expands radiating patch width
- Increases current distribution
- Enables wave propagation increase
- Reference: [7]

**Shared Coupling Between Elements:**
- Shared coupling between radiating elements within array
- Responsible for achieving wide-angle beamforming
- Applicable to both linear and phased arrays
- Reference: [8]

**Multilayer Dielectric Substrate Approach:**
- Adding multilayers of dielectric substrate
- Reference: [9]

**Circular-Polarization Waveguide Array:**
- New circular-polarization waveguide antenna array with innovative beamforming
- Design employs open quad-ridged waveguide
- Fed by straight slots cut into broad wall of rectangular waveguide
- Achieves circular polarization
- Reference: [10]

**High-Gain Millimeter-Wave Array:**
- High-gain antenna with directional pointing capability
- Integrates beamforming capabilities into single microstrip array antenna
- Operating at millimeter-wave frequencies
- Substrate: Alumina thin film
- Microstrip antenna array etched on substrate with defined dimensions and configuration
- Reference: [11]

**Tapered Antenna Array Approach:**
- Reference: [12]

**Limitations of Existing Methods:**
The methods described above enable beamforming but exhibit certain limitations:
- Complex antenna structures
- Challenges in beam scanning accuracy
- Inefficiency
- Fluctuations in antenna gain
- These issues need to be addressed for improved performance and reliability in practical applications

## 2. DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT OF MICROSTRIP PATCH ANTENNA (MPA) ARRAY

### 2.1 Overview of Proposed Design

**Configuration:**
- Microstrip patch antenna array
- Six radiating patches
- Linear arrangement
- Positioned on top of rectangular substrate

**Physical Dimensions:**
- Total occupied area: 29.5 × 7 mm
- Ground layer: Bottom of substrate

**Transmission Architecture:**
- Thin transmission lines interconnect radiating patches
- Facilitate transmission of current between patches

**Excitation Method:**
- Edge-mounted connector
- Microstrip line serves as means of excitation for linear array

**Radiation Pattern Generation:**
- Fan beam pattern radiation
- Characterized by constructive and destructive interference
- Arising from linear arrangement of radiating patches
- Interference phenomena contribute to shaping antenna's radiation pattern
- Enable directional beamforming capabilities

### 2.2 Substrate Material: RO3003

#### Material Selection Rationale

**Low Dielectric Loss Advantage:**
- RO3003 exhibits low dielectric loss
- Ensures efficient signal transmission
- Minimizes energy dissipation

**High Dielectric Constant:**
- RO3003 enables compact antenna designs
- Reduces wavelength
- Leads to smaller antenna dimensions
- Contributes to design objective of miniaturization

**Temperature and Frequency Stability:**
- Maintains stable electrical properties over wide range of frequencies
- Maintains stable electrical properties over wide range of temperatures
- Ensures consistent antenna performance in different applications
- Critical for electronic devices exposed to open environment in high temperatures

**Mechanical Stability:**
- Enhances antenna's durability
- Enhances antenna's reliability
- Crucial for long-term operation

**Circuit Integration:**
- Offers easy integration to circuit

### 2.3 Feeding Method: Microstrip Line

**Microstrip Line Feeding Selection Rationale:**

**Integration Advantages:**
- Easily integrated into antenna structure
- Simplifies overall design process

**Beamforming Control:**
- Enables precise control over phase of signals fed to each antenna element
- Facilitates beamforming and steering capabilities

**Transmission Efficiency:**
- Microstrip lines exhibit low transmission losses
- Ensures efficient signal delivery
- Maximizes antenna performance

**Compactness:**
- Contributes to overall compactness of microstrip patch antenna array
- Can be fabricated on same substrate

**Advanced Analysis Features:**

**Frequency Range Support:**

## 4. ANTENNA PERFORMANCE AND EVALUATION

### 4.1 Performance Evaluation Parameters

The designed linear MPA array is evaluated based on the following free space parameters:
- Reflection coefficient (S₁₁)
- Impedance matching
- Current distribution
- Radiation pattern
- Antenna gain

### 4.2 Reflection Coefficient (S₁₁)

**Parameter Definition:**
S₁₁ measures the amount of power reflected by the antenna relative to the power incident upon it.

**Performance Interpretation:**
- Low S₁₁ value indicates good impedance matching
- Between antenna and transmission line
- Maximizes power transfer efficiency

**Designed Antenna Performance:**
- -10 dB impedance bandwidth: 1 GHz (at resonant frequency)
- Return loss: < -16 dB
- Return loss value indicates minimal reflection within transmission line

**Performance Implication:**
Minimal reflection coefficient demonstrates:
- Excellent impedance matching
- Efficient power transfer
- Good antenna design for 50 Ω systems

### 4.3 Impedance Matching

**Feeding Method:** Microstrip line feeding

**Design Specification:** Perfect match at 50 Ω

**Performance Characteristics:**
- Antenna's input impedance matches characteristic impedance of transmission line
- Minimizes signal reflections
- Maximizes power transfer
- Maximum current from transmission line observed

**Impedance Matching Significance:**
Impedance matching ensures:
- Efficient signal delivery
- Minimal losses
- Optimized antenna performance

### 4.4 Current Distribution

**Current Distribution Analysis:**

**Progressive Wave Formation:**
- Simulated results show progressive wave pattern
- Distribution analyzed across antenna elements

**Insights Provided:**
- How electromagnetic energy is distributed
- How electromagnetic energy is radiated
- Understanding antenna's radiation characteristics
- Optimization capability for desired performance

**Physical Meaning:**
Progressive wave pattern indicates:
- Proper antenna element coupling
- Efficient power distribution
- Effective beamforming mechanism

### 4.5 Radiation Pattern

**Pattern Type:** 2D radiation pattern

**Coverage Characteristics:**
- Antenna obtained coverage in form of beam
- Good coverage in both E-plane and H-plane

**Radiation Pattern Description:**
The radiation pattern describes directional distribution of radiated electromagnetic energy from antenna.

**Key Pattern Parameters:**
- **Beamwidth:** Angular width of main radiation lobe
- **Directivity:** Concentration of radiated power
- **Sidelobe Levels:** Amplitude of secondary lobes
- **Coverage Area:** Spatial region covered by antenna
- **Spatial Focusing:** Concentration of radiation in desired direction

**Antenna Capability:**
Radiation pattern design enables:
- Directional radiation
- Beam focusing
- Spatial coverage optimization

### 4.6 Antenna Gain

**Gain Value:** 7.511 dBi (7.51 dBi)

**Gain Definition:**
Antenna gain quantifies the ratio of:
- Radiation intensity in particular direction
- Radiation intensity of isotropic radiator (radiates uniformly in all directions)

**Performance Implication:**
- Higher gain indicates greater radiation concentration
- Concentrated in desired direction
- Improves communication range
- Enhances signal strength

**Communication Benefits:**
- Extended communication range
- Improved signal quality
- Better performance for 5G applications
